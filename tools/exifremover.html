Safety Notice

Social media sites like Twitter or Instagram automatically strip EXIF data. However, if you are uploading images to personal blogs, Discord, or sending them via Email/Telegram (as file), your raw metadata is fully exposed to receivers.

Protect Your Anonymity Online

Every time you snap a photo with your smartphone or digital camera, the device stealthily embeds a hidden block of text called EXIF (Exchangeable Image File Format) Data inside the image file. While useful for organizing galleries, this metadata is a massive privacy liability. The MARK_Tools Metadata Wiper uses an HTML5 Canvas Redraw protocol to mathematically strip away all hidden tags without degrading your image's visual quality.

The Danger of OSINT

Cybercriminals and stalkers use Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) tools to extract EXIF data. A seemingly innocent photo of your cat can reveal the exact GPS coordinates (Latitude/Longitude) of your house, the exact second the photo was taken, and the specific model of iPhone or Android you own.

How Client-Side Wiping Works

Unlike apps that require you to upload your photos to a server (ironically violating your privacy to "protect" it), our tool operates strictly in your browser. It copies the raw pixels of your photo onto a blank digital canvas, discarding the "container" that holds the metadata, and repacks it locally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will removing metadata lower the photo's quality?

No. Our engine is configured to export the sanitized image at maximum quality (1.0). The visual fidelity remains identical to the original photo; only the invisible background text is destroyed.

Do social media platforms strip EXIF data automatically?

Major platforms like Instagram, Twitter (X), and Facebook strip metadata upon upload to save server space. However, if you upload the image to a personal WordPress blog, send it as a file attachment via Email, Discord, or Telegram, the receiver can download and view your full EXIF data. Always sanitize first.
