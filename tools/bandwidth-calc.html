Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What's the difference between Mbps and MB/s?

Mbps (Megabits per second) is used by ISPs for internet speed. MB/s (Megabytes per second) is used for file transfer speeds. 1 MB/s = 8 Mbps.

Why is actual download speed slower than advertised?

Our bandwidth transfer calculator provides a mathematical estimate. In reality, network overhead, server throttling, and Wi-Fi interference can reduce your actual speed by 10% to 20%.

How accurate are these calculations?

Calculations are based on standard conversion formulas. Real-world results may vary due to network conditions and throttling.

Bandwidth vs Speed Calculator: What is the difference?

Many users confuse internet speed with bandwidth. Bandwidth is the maximum capacity of your network (e.g., 100 Mbps), while internet speed is the actual transfer rate you experience. Use our data transfer calculator bandwidth tool above to estimate your real-world download times.
